Case Study: White Water Adventures Website Development

Introduction

Project Overview

Client: White Water Adventures

Project Duration: 2 months

Team: Jit Kumar Monger, Karma Dorji, Dorji Thinley, Bhumika, Nim Zam

My Role:UX research, wireframing, mockups, usability testing, and web development.

Objective

Primary Goal: To design an engaging and user-friendly website that attracts tourists to book rafting trips in Bhutan.

Research

Our journey began with an insightful interview with Mr. Tenzin, the manager at White Water Adventures. We delved deep into their vision and process, gathering essential data to guide us. This step ensured our new website would align perfectly with their needs and expectations.

Goals

  • Understand the demographic of target audiences who can be potential clients.
  • Identify features that motivate users to book rafting trips.

We crafted a comprehensive Google Forms questionnaire to survey a diverse audience about their rafting experiences and preferences. This survey aimed to gather valuable insights from various target groups, ensuring our design was user-centered and resonated with White Water Adventuresâ€™ customers.

Link to Survey Results and Secondary Research

Armed with survey data, we conducted a SWOT analysis to evaluate White Water Adventures' competitors. This analysis highlighted their strengths and areas for improvement, providing us with strategic insights to position our client favorably in the market.

Define

User Personas

From our collected data, we developed detailed personas representing our main target groups. One such persona is Sonam, a busy professional seeking a thrilling weekend escape.

Problem Statement

"Sonam needs a way to book his weekend trip so that he can take a break from his busy work schedule."

User Journey

We mapped out Sonam's journey from discovering the website to booking his trip, ensuring a seamless and satisfying experience.
